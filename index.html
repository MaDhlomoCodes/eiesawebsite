Who We Are

A Project of Nation-Building

Enough Is Enough South Africa is a youth-led civic movement refusing to watch structural failures dismantle our future. We are transforming frustration into programmatic power.

"Protest alone will not fix a broken system. We must change the rules of engagement."

Our movement arises from a collective recognition that the democratic promises made in 1994 have been compromised by institutional decay, deep corruption, and political insulation.

We see millions of young South Africans systematically excluded from economic participation, facing localized infrastructure collapses, poor water governance, and complete municipal neglect. EIE SA provides the operational backbone, civic training, and legal advocacy frameworks required to turn passive voters into an unyielding force for accountability.

By coordinating community watchdogs, driving institutional petitions, and demanding deep structural reforms, we are laying the groundwork for sustainable public integrity across all spheres of South African society.

Strategic Frontlines

Our Active Campaigns

We do not engage in rhetorical debate. We execute targeted, structured campaigns that bridge the gap between digital advocacy and physical ground-level intervention.

01 // Economic Front

Jobs for Youth (J4Y)

Active Rollout

Launched as a direct challenge to the devastating youth unemployment crisis (>60% structural exclusion), the "Jobs for Youth" campaign bypasses institutional empty talk to demand legal and industrial policy adjustments directly from local authorities and business sectors.

Our core, uncompromising programmatic demands include:

  • Enforcing a binding public and private sector youth employment demographic quota of 30-40% across all contracting firms.
  • Reorienting the Expanded Public Works Programme (EPWP) away from temporary exploitation into secure, full-time local community development roles.
  • Executing an aggressive municipal-level industrial policy designed to absorb unemployed school-leavers and graduates directly into localized production.

Immediate Action: We are compiling individual CV bundles indexed by national identification numbers to deliver directly to local Departments of Labour alongside a clear, binding Memorandum of Demands.

02 // Civic Empowerment

Your Vote, Your Power

Running Concurrent

The 2026 Local Government Elections (LGE) represent the single most powerful near-term avenue for rewriting municipal accountability. Half of South Africa sat out the 2021 municipal cycle—we are making sure that never happens again.

This initiative directly addresses systemic voter alienation through programmatic tracking:

  • Decentralized voter registration drives linking young eligible citizens straight to the Independent Electoral Commission (IEC) portals.
  • Ward-level educational workshops demystifying proportional representation, proportional seat allocations, and the independent candidate framework.
  • A massive public mobilization push demonstrating exactly how your ward councillor directly controls potholes, local electricity safety, water lines, and community support lines.
03 // Public Integrity

Transparency Now!

In Development

A campaign designed to dismantle the closed-door culture of municipal tender boards. Transparency Now! will train community members to use statutory mechanisms to audit local government spending timelines, track infrastructure delays, and publish procurement records directly to an open digital domain.

The Root Problem

Electoral Reform Campaign

The fundamental crisis of South African democracy lies in our electoral framework. Our current system insulates representatives from public consequences. We are campaigning to completely restore power to the ballot box.

The Broken Chain: Closed-List Proportional Representation

Under the current national closed-list proportional system, you do not vote for an individual human being to represent your community in Parliament. You vote for a political party logo. The party leadership then decides behind closed doors who sits on the legislature benches.

Sovereign Citizen
Casts vote for a logo
→
Party Bosses
Controls internal lists
→
Member of Parliament
Answers only to the party

This structural loop creates a devastating dynamic: public representatives are incentivized to defend their party's leadership and political survival at all costs, completely ignoring the suffering, lack of service delivery, and direct mandates of the citizens who voted for them. If an MP breaks rank to stand with the people, they are simply removed from the party list. This is what we must change.

Our Legislative Blueprint

Constitutional & Statutory Demands

EIE SA is building an alliance of legal advocates, grassroots organizations, and civil networks to lobby for a total democratic overhaul:

01

Direct Constituency-Based Representation

We demand a transition toward a constituency-focused electoral design for national and provincial chambers, mimicking our local ward structures. South Africa must be carved into explicit geographic constituencies where distinct candidates stand against one another transparently.

The Strategic Outcome: Members of Parliament will have physical constituency offices inside your neighborhood. If they fail to deliver or protect your community interests, they can be voted out directly by name in the subsequent cycle, regardless of party protection.
02

Direct Executive Election Modality

The positions of President and Provincial Premiers must be separated from legislative party seat dominance. The sovereign electorate must have a separate, dedicated ballot to vote directly for executive leadership candidates.

The Strategic Outcome: The head of state receives a direct national popular mandate from the people, creating a clear constitutional separation of powers and ensuring executive leadership answers to the general populace rather than parliament faction battles.
03

Enforceable Constituency Recall Mechanisms

Constituent majorities must possess a clear, legal avenue to initiate local recall petitions. If a sitting public representative is compromised by corruption, ethical breaches, or continuous negligence, voters can force an immediate local by-election mid-term.
